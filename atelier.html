Meet the Maker

Ralph Toledano

Trained for the Paris Opera. Making wigs by hand since 1966. Still at the bench, still finishing every piece.

For seven years, Ralph trained under master Italian wig-maker Joseph Papino, building wigs for the stages of the Paris Opera. He's spent more than 46 years at the bench since — and the house has made wigs the same way since 1966.

The Craft

How a Ralph Wig
Is Made

It begins with the hair — European, virgin, Remy, cuticle intact and all one direction, so it moves and lasts like your own. It's color-matched to you, then built onto a mesh cap hand-knotted in Japan, the world's best at it.

Then it's cut, fitted, and styled to your face by hand — and cared for after, under a one-year warranty. Seven steps, one maker, no shortcuts.
