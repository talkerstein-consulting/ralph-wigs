# Ralph — a few questions to turn on the Dealer / Trade program

The "Become a Dealer" page is built and live. Salons, sheitel machers, medical
suppliers and costume houses can already apply. To make it real, I just need
your numbers and rules. Quick answers to these and we're done — no need to
write essays, a word or a number each is fine.

## The money

1. **Wholesale / trade price** — when a salon buys to resell, what do they pay?
   (A flat % off your retail? A set price list? Roughly what margin should a
   dealer expect to keep?)

2. **Referral commission** — for the pros who'd rather just *send* the client to
   you instead of stocking: what do you pay them per sale? (% of the sale, or a
   flat amount per wig?)

3. **Minimum to start** — does a dealer have to buy a minimum first order, or a
   minimum per year? Or no minimum?

## The rules

4. **Who gets approved** — anyone with a real salon/business, or do you want to
   vet them? Any type you'd say no to?

5. **Territory / exclusivity** — do you want to promise a dealer "you're the only
   Ralph dealer in this city," or keep it open?

6. **Payment** — pay up front, or do trusted dealers get terms (e.g. pay in 30
   days)?

7. **Who ships the referral orders** — for the "refer" path, do you fit & ship to
   the client directly, or send to the salon?

## Good to know

8. **Existing dealers** — anyone already reselling for you we should protect or
   list?

9. **Warranty / returns for trade** — same 1-year warranty as retail? Can a
   dealer return unsold stock?

10. **Training** — do you want to offer dealers any guidance on fitting/selling,
    or just supply the wigs?

ready to recruit. Until then it says "trade pricing on approval," which is fine.*
