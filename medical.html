Using Your Insurance

Your Wig May Be Covered

In the U.S., a medical wig is documented as a cranial prosthesis (billing code A9282). With a doctor's prescription, many private plans reimburse part or all of the cost — and it's usually eligible for FSA and HSA funds. Nine states require insurers to cover it.

01

Get a Prescription

Ask your oncologist or dermatologist to prescribe a "cranial prosthesis" — the exact wording matters for a claim.

02

Keep Your Receipt

Hold on to your itemized receipt. Ask us to include the details your insurer looks for.

03

Submit Your Claim

Send the prescription and receipt to your insurer, FSA, or HSA for reimbursement.

General information only — not insurance or tax advice. Coverage varies by plan and state; original Medicare does not cover wigs. Verify your benefits with your insurer using the number on your card.
